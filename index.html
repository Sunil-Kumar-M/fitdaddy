Push A Chest · Triceps
Pre-Exhaust4 × 10–12
Cable Fly (Low-to-High) — PRE-EXHAUST4 × 15
Glass always pre-exhausts the chest. Set cables low, arms slightly bent, squeeze at top like hugging a barrel. No lockout.
Incline DB Press (30° Angle)4 × 10–12
30° incline only — not 45°. Elbows flared, lower to full stretch, press to just short of lockout. Keep chest loaded throughout.
Flat DB Press (Neutral Grip)4 × 10–12
Neutral grip reduces shoulder stress and keeps more tension on the pecs. Pause at the bottom for 1 sec at full stretch.
Pec Deck / Machine Fly3 × 15
Glass loves the machine fly — constant cable tension. Slow 4-sec negative, hold the contracted position for 2 sec each rep.
Overhead Cable Tricep Ext.4 × 12
Face away from cable. Arms overhead = long head fully stretched. Lower slowly, extend fully. Elbows stay pinned to ears.
Single-Arm Cable Pushdown3 × 15/arm
One arm at a time for full isolation. Rotate wrist slightly outward at bottom for peak tricep contraction. No body swing.
Tuesday · Day 02
Pull A Back · Biceps
Stretch & Contract4 × 10–12
Straight-Arm Pulldown — PRE-EXHAUST4 × 15
Isolates lat without bicep involvement. Slight forward lean, arms almost straight, pull bar to hips. Feel the lat fully load.
Wide-Grip Lat Pulldown4 × 10–12
Slight back lean, pull to upper chest. Glass emphasizes: retract scapula FIRST before pulling arms. Slow 4-sec rise on negative.
Chest-Supported DB Row4 × 12
Chest on pad eliminates lower back. Drive elbow straight up and back. Squeeze rhomboids hard at top. No shoulder shrug.
Cable Seated Row (V-Bar)3 × 12
Sit upright. At the stretch: let shoulders protract fully. At peak: pull elbows behind torso, not just to it. Hold 1 sec.

Glass staple for long-head bicep. Arm behind torso = full stretch. Supinate at top aggressively. Never let shoulders roll forward.
Single-Arm Cable Curl3 × 15/arm
Cable keeps tension at full extension unlike dumbbells. Curl across body slightly for better peak contraction. Squeeze for 2 sec.

Charles Glass · Forearm Protocol
Forearms Program

Glass trains forearms with the same precision as any other muscle. He uses full range of motion wrist curls with constant cable tension where possible, emphasises the stretch at the bottom, and trains both flexor and extensor compartments for balanced development. Hammer curls are classified here as a brachialis and brachioradialis movement. Farmer carries train grip endurance and forearm density simultaneously.
